When the last DFLC newsletter was distributed in May, the Council was gearing up for its busiest summer yet. Here are just a few of the goals achieved and milestones reached this season:
  • The Golf Committee hit a million-dollar cumulative giving milestone after its successful June tournament
  • The Outreach Committee launched www.Dana-FarberLeadershipCouncil.org
  • The Pan-Mass Challenge Committee took its first ride, exceeding its goal of raising $50,000 for Dana-Farber
What a summer! But it’s not over yet: before our fiscal year ends on September 30, we’d like you to join us in recruiting new Council members, supporting the DFLC Walk Team in any way you can, and fulfilling your annual membership commitment. The Annual Giving Committee is so close to meeting its goal of raising $350,000 with one-hundred percent member participation, and can only do so with your continued support.

We’re also excited to announce Brian Miller, MD, PhD, as the 2017-2018 DFLC Fellow. Brian earned his MD and PhD degrees at Washington University in St. Louis and is focused on tumor microenvironments and the immune response to cancer. We look forward to getting to know Brian and learning more about his work at the Fall Social.

Thank you all for your hard work thus far this year. We look forward to celebrating with you at the Fall Social on September 13!

All the best,
Jeff Beale and Deb Drapalla

Science Spotlight: Center for Adolescent and Young Adult Oncology
While survival rates for older adults and children with cancer have improved in recent decades, they have lagged for teenagers and young adults. DFCI’s Center for Adolescent and Young Adult Oncology (CAYAO) was formed to address this survival gap and better understand patients ages 15-24. “We don’t know as much about the biology of cancer in adolescents and young adults as we do in other groups, and oftentimes patients need a multidisciplinary approach,” explains Ann LaCasce, MD, MMSc, co-director of the CAYAO.

Founded in late 2016, the Center brings together experts from medical and pediatric oncology to determine optimal care for adolescent and young adult patients, researchers to develop targeted clinical trials, and psychosocial experts who understand the changes patients in this group experience. In partnership with Dana-Farber’s Young Adult Program, Just for Teens, and the Leonard P. Zakim Center for Integrative Therapies and Healthy Living, the CAYAO works to address the full spectrum of medical and emotional challenges young adults face while living with cancer.
