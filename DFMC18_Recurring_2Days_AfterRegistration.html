Essential Resources

Here are a few informative and fun resources that will be essential throughout the season:
  • Welcome Packet: Your step-by-step guide to becoming a great fundraiser is being mailed to you via USPS. The packet’s practical advice, tips, supplies, and more will help you create and implement a successful fundraising campaign. Read the online version now.

  • Website Resources: Familiarize yourself with the team website and all the resources in your online HQ. Log in now and check out all the tabs (My Webpage, Email, Reports, Tools, Community). The Community tab gives you access to fundraising support materials, the training schedule, team bulletin board, and more! You'll find online materials to supplement your Welcome Packet there, too.

  • Runners Corner: Find numerous runner resources and RSVP instructions for team meetings, group runs, and Marathon Weekend activities!

  • Bulletin Board: We look forward to hearing from you! Write a quick hello to your teammates and the Running Programs staff through the team Bulletin Board. Post questions and answers, or exchange fundraising tips with fellow teammates. Consider updating your username to make your posts easy for your teammates to recognize you (login to MyHQ and click "Edit my Login/Password").
Meet Training Advisor Jack Fultz

Jack Fultz, the 1976 Boston Marathon® winner, is DFMC's Training Advisor. He created our training schedules, which are posted in Coach’s Info, and will provide advice on various topics including nutrition, speed workouts, and apparel, via this section of each team update.

Please contact Jack with any training questions, comments, or concerns along the way. Post your inquiry in Coach's Corner, located in your Runner HQ, and Jack will be in touch with his expert advice!

"Don't know Jack" Fultz? Read more about him!

For training resources, log in to your Runner HQ and click Community > Coach's Information > Training Information.
Focus on Fundraising

This year the team aims to raise at least $5.5 million for the Claudia Adams Barr Program in Innovative Basic Cancer Research—the sole beneficiary of funds raised by the DFMC team.

Start tackling your fundraising to-do's today by:
  • Reading your Welcome Packet
  • Creating and implementing a fundraising plan
  • Writing about Barr Program impact in your fundraising appeal
  • Personalizing your RunDFMC.org web page
  • Using #RunDFMC on Twitter, Instagram, and Vine to add your posts and photos to the #RunDFMC gallery
We appreciate your dedication, and will help you every step of the way as you strive for your personal fundraising goal. In each biweekly team email, there will be a special section highlighting your individual fundraising progress so far this season. Have fun keeping track of your success!

Set your sights high, and aim to be a DFMC Pacesetter! We've established great rewards at four fundraising milestones to recognize top fundraisers throughout the season.

For fundraising resources, log in to your Runner HQ and click Community > Coach's Information.
Barr Program Impact Statements

The Barr Program impact statements found on the website provide descriptions of selected projects supported by the Barr Program. These statements are key fundraising resources, illustrating some of the most significant impacts of the Barr Program to date. We will share impact statements in every team update, as well as at group runs and meetings.

To access full materials, log in to your Runner HQ and click Community > Coach's Information > Barr Program Impact Statements.
